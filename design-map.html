Activation Regimes
Three principal configurations

Economically viable CRL configurations correspond to activation probabilities in the range of 1–3%. The table below summarises the three principal regimes identified through Monte Carlo simulation across representative asset classes.

Aggressive
z ≈ 1.8
pevent ≈ 5–8%
High activation frequency. Trigger is reached often. Suitable where frequent payoff events are structurally intended by the issuer.
Standard
z ≈ 2.2
pevent ≈ 2–3%
Balanced economic structure. Primary design target for most CRL configurations across asset classes and time horizons.
Rare Event
z ≈ 2.6
pevent ≈ 1%
Structurally selective. Trigger is reached rarely. Suitable for high-conviction, event-driven configurations.

Note. In practice, empirical distributions, jump risk, and volatility clustering introduce deviations from the idealised GBM-based mapping between z and pevent. These values should be treated as reference ranges. Full calibration requires Monte Carlo simulation over empirically observed return distributions.
