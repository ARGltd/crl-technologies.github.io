™ Understanding the payoff

Early isn't the same as wrong.

With standard leverage, a stop-loss at the same price can translate into very different equity losses depending on when the market moves against you. CRL changes this by separating timing from direction.

Same stop price. Smaller equity loss before confirmation.

The timing problem

"I was right, but I got stopped out."

This isn't bad luck. It's how traditional leverage works.

Standard leverage amplifies from the first tick. It doesn't know if you're wrong about direction or just early on timing. It punishes both the same way: immediate, full exposure.

The result: many traders get forced out of positions that would have worked — not because their thesis was wrong, but because the market moved against them before confirming their view.

How CRL changes the leverage profile

Standard leverage
Full exposure from entry
Leverage applies immediately.
A -10% move at 5× can mean a much larger equity hit.
CRL profile
Base until confirmed
Base exposure before confirmation.
Leverage activates after confirmation.

CRL doesn't predict the market. It doesn't remove risk. It changes when leverage amplification activates — giving the market time to confirm your direction before full exposure applies.

What CRL does not do

✕
Predict market direction

CRL doesn't know where the market is going. You still need to be right about direction.

✕
Eliminate risk

You can still lose money — including any premium (issuer-defined) charged by your broker for CRL-enabled products.

✕
Guarantee profits

If the trigger is never hit, you don't get leveraged returns — even if the market eventually moves your way.

CRL changes the timing of leverage activation. That's it. It's a structural difference, not a magic solution.
